# INV3 — the six claims, graded

> Central claim audited: *"particles, mass, energy, time, gravity and motion are
> macroscopic descriptions of the same microscopic causal network."*
> Grades: **A** = calculated and verified · **B** = calculated, not rigorously verified
> · **C** = physical argument, no numerical calculation · **D** = reinterpretation of
> prior results (no new calculation).

| # | claim | what a proof needs | what T14–T21 did | grade |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| (i) | **particles** = causal connectivity | stable localized states with definite mass/charge/spin obeying Dirac/Klein–Gordon | hand-built helices/braids; spectral clustering separates the **imposed** classes (T21E); no dynamical stability, no conserved quantum number verified, no wave equation | **B→C** |

## Reading
- **No claim reaches A.** None is both calculated and rigorously verified.
- Two are **D** (reinterpretation): time (iv) and gravity (v) are TEIC's already-established
  R1–R3 / D1–D3, re-expressed in connectivity language and, where re-computed, with **less**
  rigor than the originals.
- Two are **B** (calculated, unverified): mass (ii) and the existence of localized
  structures (i) — real measurements, but near-tautological (mass) or of imposed
  structures (particles), with the decisive tests (Lorentz invariance, dynamics, quantum
  numbers) absent.
- Two are **C** (argument): energy (iii) and motion (vi) are physical narratives with
  definitional or no numerics behind them.

The honest one-line grade of the central claim: **the direction is plausible and partly
motivated, but the six-fold "is" is supported as at most one B and several C/D — not the
"validated empirically" the `RESUMO_FINAL` files assert.**
