# A4 — Critique of Gemini's conclusion "TEIC is an independent framework"

## The three readings

**(i) Strong — TEIC and DEV are disconnected.**
→ **False**, and Gemini does **not** actually claim this. The minimal action produces
precisely the DEV operator set ($X$, $A^2$, $A\!\cdot\!\partial\theta$, $F^2$, DBI), as
both Gemini (T3/T5) and TEIC (C1–C4, W1–W4) find. Disconnected is refuted by both.

**(ii) Medium — TEIC produces a FAMILY of EFTs; DEV is one member.**
→ **This is exactly what Gemini means.** EXECUTIVE_SUMMARY: "A TEIC fundamenta uma
classe de teorias de Proca-Maxwell anisotrópicas. A DEV (covariante e sem massa) é um
ponto de alta simetria (caso especial)… mas não o mais natural." T5: "DEV é o limite
covariante ($LV\to0$) e de massa nula ($m_A\to0$)." This **matches TEIC's own
conclusion**: C2 — clean ratios but ≠ generic DEV, the **gauge-invariant Proca/
Stückelberg special case** (a "sister theory"); W4 — "form-complete; DEV is the
Lorentz-invariant point; the gauge weight $\lambda_p$ is free."

**(iii) Weak — TEIC is the ORIGIN of DEV, not a reduction of it.**
→ **Correct**, and compatible with (ii). TEIC does not derive *from* DEV; it *generates*
DEV (as the symmetric point of its EFT tree). If Gemini meant this, the only issue is the
**word**: "independente" understates the relationship. The accurate word is
**"fundamental / microscopic origin"**, not "independent."

## What Gemini got right vs the wording problem

Substantively, Gemini's conclusion = TEIC's conclusion: **DEV is a high-symmetry special
case of a more general (Proca–Maxwell–LV) structure that TEIC generates.** That is
readings (ii)+(iii), and it is **correct**.

The **error is only lexical**: "independent framework" suggests reading (i)
(disconnected), which is false and which Gemini's own text contradicts. A precise
restatement: *"TEIC is the microscopic origin of a Proca–Maxwell–scalar EFT family of
which DEV is the Lorentz-invariant, massless special case."*

## The one genuinely divergent claim — and why it is over-generalised

`teic_st` T12H states **"DEV NÃO EMERGE… TEIC-ST é fenomenologicamente inferior à DEV"**
(Keplerian rotation curves, Tully-Fisher slope 0.5 not 0.25). This is a real result —
**but about a truncation**: the `teic_st` phase models TEIC as a **scalar-tensor
Brans–Dicke** theory $f(\theta)R=(1+\theta)R$ (ST2), **dropping the vector/Wilson
sector**. TEIC's bridge work shows the **vector (Proca/Stückelberg/plaquette) sector is
what carries the gravitational modification**. So "DEV inferior" is true *for the
scalar-only reduction* and is **over-generalised** in T12H to all of TEIC. It does **not**
refute the full-TEIC→DEV bridge (which is form-complete with the vector sector included).

## Recommendation

- Gemini's framing is **right in substance** (DEV = special case of a TEIC-generated EFT
  family) — accept readings (ii)/(iii); reject the misleading word "independent."
- Gemini's "DEV inferior" is **scope-limited** (scalar-tensor truncation, vector sector
  dropped) and should not be read as a verdict on full TEIC.
