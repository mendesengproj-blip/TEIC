## What exists

1. **Gauge fields on causal sets** — Sverdlov, *Gauge Fields in Causal Set Theory*
   (arXiv:0807.2066, 2008) and Sverdlov–Bombelli, *Dynamics for causal sets with matter
   fields* (arXiv:0905.1506, 2009). Build U(1)/Yang–Mills **holonomy actions** on causal
   diamonds, and couple **matter (scalar/spinor) fields** to them. This is the conceptual
   ancestor of E5/E6/E7. They do NOT build a smoothed BD gauge **operator**, and do not
   couple an **order-parameter / sigma-model field** to the gauge bivector.

2. **Order-parameter–gauge coupling in condensed matter** — the physical template the prompt
   invokes: a condensate ⟨φ⟩ coupled to a U(1) gauge field gives the **Meissner/Anderson–
   Higgs** mechanism (London 1935; Anderson 1963; Higgs 1964). The condensate's PHASE
   stiffness gives the photon a mass and expels magnetic flux. Crucially, what couples is the
   **coherent order parameter**, not the disordered fluctuations. In a ferromagnet the analog
   is the **spin stiffness** of the ordered magnetization; spin–orbit / Dzyaloshinskii–Moriya
   terms `D·(S_i×S_j)` produce magnetization currents and chiral textures (skyrmions).

3. **TEIC's own ferromagnet** — E1 (O(3)/Heisenberg orientation field n⃗∈S² on the events)
   orders spontaneously (2nd-order, J_c≈0.08 on the box graph); E2 measures its magnons.
   The cross term `(n_i×n_j)·ê_z` in the prompt is precisely a DM-like / magnetization-current
   coupling of the ordered texture to the link (gauge) variable.

## The gap E6d targets, and the honest precedent caveat

No CST work couples the **orientation order parameter** to the **gauge area-bivector E/B
split**, nor asks whether such a coupling AMPLIFIES a curvature-induced magnetic sector.
E6d is therefore novel as a measurement.

But the condensed-matter precedent already carries a **warning the result must respect**:
the Meissner/Higgs amplification is sourced by the **coherent** condensate, whereas a sum of
squares like `b² = Σ(Ã^{ij})²` is inflated by **any** internal field variance — including
**incoherent (disordered)** texture. So the decisive test is not "does frac_B rise with λ"
(it generically will, from added spacelike variance) but "does the rise REQUIRE order"
(gate G2). The literature predicts that **coherent** order sweeps LITTLE internal bivector
area (neighbouring spins nearly parallel → small `n_i×n_j`), while **disordered** texture
sweeps a lot — i.e. the naive coupling is expected to be *anti*-amplifying in the ordered
phase. E6d tests this directly with the pre-registered G2 gate.
