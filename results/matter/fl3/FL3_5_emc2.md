# FL3-5 — Connection with E = mc²

FL3-5 was pre-registered as *conditional on creation*. No creation occurred, so there is no
positive E=mc² confirmation to report. But the threshold relation is exactly **why** no
creation occurred, so it is the central quantitative result, evaluated in the negative.

## The condition
Creating an extra Skyrmion + anti-Skyrmion pair costs two new rest masses of topological
energy:
$$E_{\rm collision} \;\ge\; 2\,M_{\rm Sk}\,c^2 .$$
Each quantity is sourced independently, with no circularity:

| symbol | value | source (anti-circular) |
|---|---|---|
| M_Sk | 285.5 | 3D lattice energy of the relaxed Skyrmion (`fl3_core.lattice_mass`, SU3 functional) |
| c | 0.9797 | magnon group speed MEASURED in E2 dispersion (ω = ck) |
| 2 M_Sk c² | **548.1** | threshold |
| E_collision (KE₀, v=0.5c) | **10.11** | finite-difference boost kinetic energy |

## The result
$$\frac{E_{\rm collision}}{2 M_{\rm Sk} c^2} = \frac{10.11}{548.1} = 0.018 .$$

The frontal v=0.5c collision carries **1.8%** of the energy required to create a pair; even
v=0.7c reaches only 4%. The inequality fails by a factor of ~25–55 across the charter grid.

## Reading
This is E=mc² acting as a **conservation law that forbids creation**, not a fitted
coincidence: c and M_Sk emerge from the *same* causal lattice (the magnon light cone of E2
and the Skyrme energy functional of SU3), and their product sets a rest-mass cost that a
sub-relativistic collision simply cannot pay. The two solitons therefore annihilate into
magnon radiation (FL3-2/FL3-3) — the energy that was bound in their topological gradients
is released as on-shell magnons (E2 quanta), exactly the time-reverse of what creation would
have required. So FL3-5 confirms E=mc² in the only way this energy budget allows: the
threshold is real, it is set by lattice-derived c and M_Sk, and it is not met.

(The boosted lattice profile additionally radiates part of its momentum in flight, so the
*effective* collision energy is below KE₀ — the gap to threshold is if anything larger than
the table shows.)
