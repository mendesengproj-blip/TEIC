# Cover letter — Physical Review D

To the Editors of *Physical Review D*,

I submit for your consideration the manuscript **"Colour ferromagnetism in a
causal network: confinement, the meson octet, and the order of the transition."**

**What the paper does.** Causal set theory recovers Lorentz invariance and
spacetime dimension from a Poisson sprinkling of events with their causal order.
The paper asks whether a non-Abelian colour sector—spontaneous ordering,
confinement, and a meson spectrum—can emerge on that same substrate, without
inserting any quantum-chromodynamics input into the data-generating code.
Assigning an $SU(3)$ element to each event and coupling causal neighbours by the
minimal principal-chiral density $-\tfrac13\,\mathrm{Re}\,\mathrm{Tr}(U_iU_j^\dagger)$,
we establish four results by measurement, all under a mechanically enforced
anti-circularity guard.

**The four results.** (i) The vacuum orders spontaneously into a *colour
ferromagnet*—decisively on the Poisson causal substrate at $J_c\simeq0.3$, with
genuine long-range order ($C_\infty=m^2$, Mermin clustering to $1$–$3\%$)—and the
same vacuum supports a stable colour Skyrmion [$\pi_3(SU(3))=\mathbb{Z}$, $B=+1$]
and a confining static potential ($\sigma>0$ from the Creutz ratio, decreasing
with the bare coupling). (ii) The eight broken-generator Goldstone modes form one
exactly degenerate octet (per-generator spread $4\times10^{-8}$), with a single
stiffness and speed $c=\sqrt{2/3}$—the pseudoscalar meson octet, forced by the
unbroken $SU(3)_V$ adjoint, not postulated. (iii) All three survive a $\pm10\%$
deformation of the action. (iv) Characterising the order of the transition over
$L=8$–$24$ excludes a clean second-order transition ($\chi_{\max}$ grows at least
as the volume, the Binder dip deepens without saturating); a weak first-order
transition is the most probable reading, reinforced at $L=32$ by parallel
tempering (bimodal energy histogram, a sharp susceptibility step, a
replica-exchange bottleneck at $J_c$).

**What we are careful to claim.** All dimensionful scales remain external: the
substrate fixes forms and dimensionless ratios, not the lattice-to-GeV scale. We
report the transition order honestly as weak-versus-strong-first-order *open*: the
latent heat stays below resolution because the tempering does not equilibrate
across the barrier, and we state the requirement ($L\gtrsim48$ with multicanonical
sampling on a cluster) rather than overclaim.

**Why PRD.** The work concerns non-Abelian gauge/matter phenomena—colour ordering,
confinement from a Creutz ratio, a topological baryon, a Goldstone meson
spectrum—on a discrete quantum-gravity substrate, within the journal's scope
(gravitation; particles and fields on discrete structures; lattice methods). The
anti-circularity protocol, the validation gates, and the finite-size-scaling
criteria are part of the build and are described so the claims can be checked
before any recomputation.

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and reports the
author's own simulations; the figures are generated from the same runs that
produce the quoted numbers. I declare no conflicts of interest.

Thank you for considering the manuscript.
