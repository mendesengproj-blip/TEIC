# Cover letter — Physical Review D

To the Editors of *Physical Review D*,

I submit for your consideration the manuscript **"Spontaneous orientational order on
a causal set and its relativistic scalar Goldstone modes."**

**What the paper does.** Causal set theory recovers Lorentz invariance and spacetime
dimension from a Poisson sprinkling of events with their causal order. We equip that
same substrate with one internal degree of freedom—an $O(3)$ orientation on each
event—and ask, under a mechanically enforced protocol that forbids any relativistic
or quantum expression in the data-generating code, what its ordered vacuum and its
Goldstone sector are. We establish three results by measurement: (i) the orientation
field spontaneously orders, and a finite-size-scaling analysis shows the order is
genuine (the order parameter rises with system size, Binder cumulant $U_4=2/3$); (ii)
a field propagated with the Sorkin–Benincasa–Dowker causal wave operator disperses
as $\omega=ck$, with the light-cone speed recovered without being inserted; and (iii)
we test directly whether the two transverse Goldstone modes are an emergent gauge
vector (photon) or two decoupled internal scalars.

**The central result is a measured negative, reported as such.** Using a polarisation
tensor over 160 wavevectors and a confound-free permutation null, we find that the
polarisation does *not* lock to the spatial wavevector (circular correlation $0.10$,
permutation $p=0.23$): the modes are two degenerate, spatially isotropic *internal
scalars*, not a transverse gauge vector. We had conjectured the photon identification
on the strength of the mode count and the relativistic dispersion; this measurement
**excludes it, and we retract it**. The outcome is internally consistent with the
model's $SU(3)$ extension, where the broken-generator Goldstones are pseudoscalar
mesons, and it points—by elimination—to the gauge-connection (link-variable) sector
as where an emergent photon would have to originate.

**Why PRD.** The manuscript concerns emergent gauge/Goldstone phenomena on a discrete
quantum-gravity substrate—causal set theory, the causal d'Alembertian, lattice field
theory—within the journal's scope (gravitation; particles and fields on discrete
structures). We believe the result is of interest precisely because it is decisive
and honest: it sharpens, by pre-registered measurement, what a spontaneously ordered
causal vacuum does and does not produce, and corrects a natural but unsupported
identification rather than asserting it. The anti-circularity protocol, the
validation gates, and the pre-registered decision criteria are part of the build and
are described so the central claims can be checked before any recomputation.

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and reports the
author's own simulations; the figures are generated from the same runs that produce
the quoted numbers. I declare no conflicts of interest.

Thank you for considering the manuscript.
