# Cover letter — Physical Review Letters

To the Editors of *Physical Review Letters*,

I submit for your consideration the manuscript **"Dimensionless structure across
four domains of a causal network: forms derive, absolute scales do not."**

**What the paper does.** Emergent-spacetime programmes are often read as promising
to derive "everything." This Letter makes and tests a deliberately narrower,
falsifiable statement. A single discrete causal substrate—a Poisson sprinkling of
events with their causal order, equipped with one internal field and analysed under
a mechanically enforced anti-circularity guard—reproduces the *dimensionless*
structure of physics in four domains that were not built to agree: emergent
Newtonian gravity, a spin-$\tfrac12$ baryon with $SU(3)$ colour confinement, the
deep-MOND interpolation, and the order parameter of objective wave-function
collapse. In each domain the substrate also fixes *internal* scales in lattice
units, but never the conversion of those units to SI, so $G$, $\hbar$, $a_0$,
$f_\pi$ and the collapse threshold $\eta$ each require exactly one external
calibration.

**The central claim, and why it is decisive.** The pattern is the lattice-QCD
situation with one honest difference: lattice QCD manufactures its scale
$\Lambda_{\rm QCD}$ from a dimensionless coupling by dimensional transmutation,
whereas here no transmutation operates. We tested the cheapest equilibrium
signature—a divergent correlation length at the ordering transition—and the
substrate has none (mean-field, in equilibrium and under a non-equilibrium
parametric drive), for a structural reason: for any Lorentz-invariant connection
rule on the sprinkling the expected coordination diverges with system size, so no
critical length survives to anchor a manufactured scale. The claim is falsifiable
by a single counterexample: one domain in which the lattice$\to$SI conversion is
fixed by the substrate with no external input would refute it.

**What we are careful to claim.** We report plainly where the evidence is weak (the
cosmology link) and where it is convergence rather than derivation (the collapse
domain), rather than presenting a uniform front. The unifying statement is
structural—which quantities the substrate can and cannot fix—not a claim to
reproduce Nature in detail.

**Why PRL.** The result is a single, broadly significant, and sharply falsifiable
structural statement that ties together four normally separate areas—gravitation,
non-Abelian gauge matter, galaxy dynamics, and foundations of quantum
mechanics—through one mechanism (Lorentz-invariant discreteness forbids an
anchoring scale). Its interest is precisely its breadth combined with a stated kill
criterion, which we believe suits a Letter.

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and reports the
author's own simulations except where it explicitly converges with independent
work; the figures are generated from the same runs that produce the quoted numbers.
I declare no conflicts of interest.

Thank you for considering the manuscript.
