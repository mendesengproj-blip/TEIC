# Cover letter — MNRAS

To the Editors of *Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society*,

I submit for your consideration the manuscript **"Does the radial-acceleration
scale evolve? A pre-registered test of $a_0 \propto H(z)$ with the baryonic
Tully–Fisher relation, and first constraints at $z\sim1$."**

**What the paper does.** The radial acceleration relation (RAR) is governed by a
characteristic scale $a_0\simeq1.2\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$. Whether that
scale is constant in cosmic time is an open, observationally decidable question that
separates the $\Lambda$CDM expectation (a scale set by slowly varying
galaxy-formation physics) from a class of modified-dynamics theories that tie it to
the Hubble rate. We derive, from the baryonic Tully–Fisher relation and the
hypothesis $a_0\propto H(z)$, the parameter-free prediction
$\Delta\log v_{\rm flat}=\tfrac14\log_{10}[H(z)/H_0]$, and we present a complete,
**pre-registered** test protocol: sample-selection criteria isolating the
low-acceleration regime, a two-component error model separating the statistical term
from a non-averaging zero-point systematic floor, a transparent significance
estimator, a detection forecast, and a verbatim kill criterion. Applying the
unmodified protocol to the first public catalogue in the correct regime
(MUSE-DARK III), we measure the critical acceleration rising with redshift at
$\ge15\sigma$, with an amplitude consistent with the prediction at $0.5$–$0.9\sigma$
on the independent SPARC anchor, while a redshift-independent RAR is disfavoured at
$\sim19\sigma$.

**What we are careful to claim.** We report this explicitly as *evidence consistent
with, not confirmation of*, the prediction: the decisive $z\ge2$ regime is
unmeasured (the kill criterion is armed but not yet executable), the functional form
of the rise is undecided, and an independent mass-axis analysis of the same epoch
finds no evolution—a discordance we report rather than adjudicate. A dedicated
section discusses robustness to local anchors, cosmology, and unmodelled systematics
(progenitor/population matching, flux-limited selection, baryonic-mass calibration,
kinematic modelling), and we are candid about the present single-catalogue
dependence, mitigated by the catalogue-independent protocol and forecast that are
the paper's primary methodological contribution.

**Why MNRAS.** The work is observational galaxy kinematics and cosmology—RAR/BTFR
measurement, statistical methodology, and a falsifiable forecast for SKA/MeerKAT and
JWST/ALMA—squarely within the journal's scope. The test is deliberately
framework-agnostic: it can be evaluated, and is useful, independently of any
underlying theory of why $a_0$ might track $H(z)$; we treat $a_0\propto H(z)$ as a
phenomenological ansatz to be confronted with data.

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and uses only
published catalogues (MUSE-DARK III, SPARC, MIGHTEE-HI, KMOS$^{3\rm D}$, Gaia DR3
wide-binary analyses); the forecast and confrontation are reproducible from the
equations in the Methods section. I declare no conflicts of interest.

Thank you for considering the manuscript.
