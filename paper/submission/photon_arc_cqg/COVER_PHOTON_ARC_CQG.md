# Cover letter — Classical and Quantum Gravity

To the Editors of *Classical and Quantum Gravity*,

I submit for your consideration the manuscript **"Where, and why, a photon is hard
to emerge in causal set theory: a systematic map and an indefinite-signature gauge
operator."**

**What the paper does.** A recurring hope in emergent-spacetime programmes is that
the photon—a massless transverse gauge vector with a Coulomb phase—should arise
from the substrate rather than be postulated. The paper reports a systematic,
pre-registered search for such a photon on a causal set (a Poisson sprinkling with
its causal order). The result is an *informative negative*: a map of exactly where
and why the photon is hard, ending at a precisely stated open construction. The
manuscript is deliberately framed as a negative result, and its contribution is the
map plus a new tool, not a claimed photon.

**The six measured links.** (i) The Goldstone modes of an internal orientation
order are two degenerate internal *scalars*, not a gauge vector (their polarisation
does not lock to the wavevector, permutation $p=0.23$). (ii) Relocating the question
to the link-$U(1)$ connection sector, the bare connection inherits the causal set's
non-locality and the Coulomb phase is not certified. (iii) We construct an
indefinite-signature ($E^2-B^2$) Benincasa–Dowker gauge operator—absent from the
causal-set literature, which carries the d'Alembertian only on scalars—that is
gauge-invariant to $5.7\times10^{-16}$ and reproduces free Maxwell on a 4D lattice
($\omega=ck$, $c=1.05$); but on the causet it fails, because causal diamonds are
$100\%$ electric (the magnetic sector is structurally empty). (iv) Diamond height
does not supply the missing spacelike cell. (v) Spatial curvature does: the
magnetic-cell fraction rises monotonically and crosses $0.01$ at de Sitter radius
$\hat R=2$. (vi) Coupling the ordered ferromagnet to the gauge field does not
amplify it.

**What we conclude, carefully.** Extrapolating the one positive lever,
$\Delta f_B\propto(1/\hat R)^{1.73}\approx H^2$ ($R^2=0.997$), an $O(10\%)$ magnetic
sector requires sub-Planckian curvature. The photon mechanism is therefore real but
Planck-scale, and does not explain the photons of the observable low-curvature
universe. Two further low-curvature levers (future-cone cells and a Bianchi-I
anisotropy on the causal order) are exhausted as well, closing the low-curvature
map. We state the remaining obstruction exactly: a Lorentz-invariant spacelike
2-cell on the causal order at low curvature.

**Why CQG.** The work is causal set theory and discrete gauge structure on a
quantum-gravity substrate—a new indefinite-signature Benincasa–Dowker operator, the
causal d'Alembertian, curvature dependence of an emergent magnetic sector—squarely
within the journal's scope. The value is in a decisive, honestly reported map: it
tells the community precisely where an emergent photon must be sought and why the
obvious routes fail, with a reusable gauge operator as a byproduct.
