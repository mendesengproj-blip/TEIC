# Cover letter — Physical Review D

To the Editors of *Physical Review D*,

I submit for your consideration the manuscript **"Topological matter and emergent
gravitation from a causal network: a soliton that sources its own gravitational
field."**

**What the paper does.** Causal set theory recovers Lorentz invariance and
spacetime dimension from a Poisson sprinkling of events with their causal order.
The paper asks whether that same substrate, carrying one internal orientation
field, can produce—simultaneously and from the same construction—stable matter
with the correct quantum statistics *and* the gravitational field that matter
should generate. Everything is measured under a mechanically enforced guard that
forbids any relativistic expression in the data-generating code.

**The measured links.** (i) Under the Benincasa–Dowker action with a localised
source and no imposed profile, the coarse-grained causal-density field relaxes to
$\theta\propto r^{-1.02}$ (exterior exponent $-1.00$ for every source shape tested,
matching an independent Gauss-law quadrature to $0.9998$); superposition is exact
for the linear action, and a non-circular chain-counting measurement of the
Schwarzschild dilation reproduces $\sqrt{1-2M/r}$ to $0.2\%$. (ii) Among candidate
internal groups only $SU(2)$ admits a conserved, localised, finite-energy
topological charge; $U(1)$ is excluded by $\pi_2(S^1)=0$, by theorem and by
measurement. (iii) A pointwise Cauchy–Schwarz bound $K\le\tfrac23 S$ (verified
against $10^6$ adversarial configurations) proves the Skyrme stabiliser cannot
dominate spontaneously and is therefore an external input, declared as such.
(iv) The $SU(2)$ soliton exists with $B=1$ and measured spin-$\tfrac12$ fermionic
statistics (Finkelstein–Rubinstein constraint, exchange phase = $2\pi$-rotation
phase). (v) Quantising the soliton's collective rotation, a *single* calibration
(the $N$–$\Delta$ splitting) fixes the one dimensionless coupling to $e=5.39$ ($1\%$
from Adkins–Nappi–Witten), after which $\mu_p/\mu_n=-1.51$ (experiment $-1.46$),
the isoscalar radius, and the multiplet degeneracies are parameter-free.

**The central result.** Feeding the soliton's *own* relaxed energy-density profile
as the literal source into the same Benincasa–Dowker relaxer yields
$\theta=G_{\rm net}M/r$ with exterior exponent $-0.992$ and $G_{\rm net}=A/M$
constant to five digits across the soliton mass. Matter and gravity are one
measurement, not two coincident ones. We are explicit about the boundary: the
*form* is derived; the absolute coupling $G_{\rm net}\propto1/K$ and the GeV scale
($f_\pi$) are external, and the Skyrme stabiliser is imported with a proof of why
it must be.

**Why PRD.** The work is gravitation and field theory on a discrete substrate—an
emergent Newtonian/Schwarzschild sector, a topological soliton with baryon
phenomenology, and the direct sourcing of geometry by matter—squarely within the
journal's scope. The anti-circularity guard and the validation gates are part of
the build and are described so the claims can be checked before any recomputation.

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and reports the
author's own simulations; the figures are generated from the same runs that
produce the quoted numbers. I declare no conflicts of interest.

Thank you for considering the manuscript.
